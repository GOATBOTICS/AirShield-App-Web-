Air Quality Parameters

These are the main pollutants measured to determine air quality. Lower values are better.

PM2.5 (Fine Particles)

Tiny particles from smoke and exhaust. They can enter the bloodstream and are a major health concern.

Good: 0-12 Moderate: 12-35 Unhealthy: 35+

PM10 (Coarse Particles)

Larger particles like dust and pollen that can irritate the respiratory system.

Good: 0-54 Moderate: 54-154 Unhealthy: 154+

NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide)

A gas mainly from vehicle traffic. High levels can aggravate respiratory diseases like asthma.

O₃ (Ground-Level Ozone)

The main component of smog, created when pollutants react with sunlight. It can reduce lung function.
